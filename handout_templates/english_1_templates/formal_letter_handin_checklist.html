Formal Letter Hand-in Checklist

Tick all boxes before you hand in your text

Task & audience

  • I follow the assignment brief and word limit.
  • I write to a principal/headteacher as a formal audience.

Structure

  • Subject line states the topic neutrally.
  • Greeting: "Dear Principal/Headteacher [Surname],"
  • Opening sentence gives purpose in one clear line.
  • Early position: state your view in one sentence.
  • Body: 2–3 short paragraphs, each with one main point and a fact/example.
  • Solutions: suggest 2–3 realistic actions and a benefit.
  • Conclusion: restate view + polite call to action (reply, meeting, next step).
  • Sign-off: "Yours sincerely," + full name + class.

Tone & register

  • Formal and polite. No slang, emojis, or threats.
  • Use polite requests: "Could…," "I would appreciate…"
  • Avoid direct blame; focus on problems and solutions.

Coherence

  • Use clear linkers: "because," "therefore," "however," "for example."
  • One idea per paragraph; short sentences.

Sentence control

  • No run-ons or comma splices. Split long sentences.
  • Average sentence length ~12–20 words.

Grammar

  • Subject–verb agreement: "students are," "stress is," "the school needs."
  • Tense is consistent.
  • Modal + base verb: "should reduce," "could provide."
  • Articles: a/an/the are used correctly.
  • Prepositions: "at school," "on Monday," "in the morning."

Spelling, punctuation, capitalization

  • Capitalize "I," names, titles, and sentence starts.
  • Apostrophes: possession vs contractions are correct.
  • Full stops end complete sentences.

Common word traps

  • its / it's
  • their / there / they're
  • to / too
  • affect (verb) / effect (noun)

Clarity & data

  • Times, dates, and quantities are specific where used.
  • Staff are addressed by title and surname only.

Final pass

  • Read once for task/tone, once for sentences, once for grammar/spelling.
  • Run spell-check set to English.
