Debate Card — Pro Choice vs Pro Life

Motion + prep facts on one page. English only here; Swedish glossary after the page break.

Motion

Should the law prioritize a person's right to choose over the potential life of a fetus?

Context

The abortion debate involves a profound conflict of moral principles. At its heart is the clash between two fundamental values: the right of an individual to control their own body (bodily autonomy) and the belief that a fetus has a right to life from conception.

The pro-choice position argues that forcing someone to carry a pregnancy against their will is a violation of their basic human rights. The pro-life position argues that a fetus is a distinct human life and that ending it is a moral wrong. This debate forces society to ask when life begins and whose rights should prevail when they are in conflict.

Key questions

  • When does life begin? At what point—conception, heartbeat, brain activity, or birth—should a fetus be granted the legal rights of a person?
  • Bodily Autonomy vs. Right to Life: Does a person's right to control their own body override the potential right to life of a fetus?
  • The Role of the State: What is the government's proper role: to protect individual liberty by ensuring access to abortion, or to protect potential life by restricting it?

PRO-CHOICE — Prioritize Autonomy

  • Bodily Autonomy is a Foundational Right: Every person has the absolute right to make decisions about their own body. Forcing someone to carry a pregnancy is a profound violation of this right.
  • A Fetus is Not a Person: A fetus, especially in early stages, lacks consciousness and cannot survive on its own. It is part of the pregnant person's body and should not have superseding rights.
  • Societal Well-being: Access to safe, legal abortion is essential for gender equality, economic stability, and public health. Banning it does not stop abortions; it only stops safe ones.

PRO-LIFE — Prioritize the Fetus

  • Life Begins at Conception: A fetus is a unique human being with its own DNA from the moment of conception. It has a fundamental right to life that must be protected.
  • The Unborn Cannot Speak for Themselves: The most vulnerable members of society deserve the most protection. A fetus is a defenseless human being who cannot advocate for its own right to exist.
  • Abortion is an Act of Violence: The procedure intentionally ends a human life. Society has a moral duty to prohibit acts that destroy human life, and instead promote alternatives like adoption.

POIs to test the other side

  • If a fetus has a right to life, must the state also force people to donate organs to save a life?
  • If bodily autonomy is absolute, should there be any time limit on abortion, even moments before birth?
  • If life begins at conception, should the practice of creating and discarding excess embryos in IVF be illegal?
  • Since a fetus has a unique human genetic code from conception, why is it not a human life deserving protection?

Glossary — English → Swedish

Short definitions to support understanding. Use English terms in the debate.

bodily autonomy → kroppslig autonomi — principen att varje individ har en absolut rätt att bestämma över sin egen kropp. Kärnan i pro-choice-argumentet.

personhood → personstatus — den etiska och juridiska frågan om när ett foster ska betraktas som en person med fullständiga rättigheter, inklusive rätten till liv.

right to life → rätt till liv — principen att en människa har en grundläggande rätt att leva och inte bli dödad. Kärnan i pro-life-argumentet, som menar att denna rättighet börjar vid befruktningen.

sanctity of life → livets okränkbarhet — den moraliska eller religiösa övertygelsen att livet är heligt från befruktning till naturlig död och därför måste skyddas.

viability → livsduglighet — tidpunkten då ett foster kan överleva utanför livmodern. Används ofta som ett argument för en möjlig gräns för abort.

conception → befruktning — ögonblicket då en spermie befruktar ett ägg och en unik genetisk kod skapas. Många i pro-life-rörelsen anser att livet börjar här.

access to care → tillgång till vård — att alla, oavsett var de bor eller hur mycket pengar de har, ska ha faktisk möjlighet att få en säker och laglig abort.

pro-choice → för valfrihet — ståndpunkten att en gravid person, inte staten, ska ha den slutliga rätten att bestämma över en graviditet.

pro-life → för livet — ståndpunkten att ett foster är en människa med rätt till liv, och att abort därför är moraliskt fel och bör vara olagligt.
