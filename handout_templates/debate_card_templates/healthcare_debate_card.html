Debate Card — Age Priority in Health Care

Motion + prep facts on one page. English only here; Swedish glossary after the page break.

Motion

Should hospitals prioritize younger patients over older patients for life-saving treatment during a severe shortage?

Context

During a crisis, intensive-care beds, ventilators, or critical drugs may be too few for everyone who needs them.

Policy must explain who is treated first. Some argue the aim should be to save the most years of life, while others insist that age alone should never decide access to care.

Clinical guidelines still require medical need and expected benefit as the first filters; if age is used at all, it must stay a narrow rule.

Key questions

  • More life-years vs equal respect: Should saving the most life-years outweigh treating all ages the same?
  • Rule clarity vs clinical judgment: Is a bright-line rule (“younger first”) better than bedside prognosis?
  • Tie-breaker or full rule: May age only act once need and prognosis are tied, or can it become a stand-alone rule?

YES — Prioritise the young

  • Maximise life-years: When equipment is scarce, giving it to those likely to live longer can increase total life-years saved.
  • Fair chance to live a full life: Younger patients have experienced fewer life stages, so using age as a tie-breaker can be a proportional response.
  • Predictable rules: Public criteria avoid first-come, first-served chaos and give doctors cover to follow agreed priorities.

NO — Keep age out of healthcare

  • Equal human dignity: The human dignity principle says age alone must never decide who gets care.
  • Clinical relevance first: Frailty, comorbidities, and prognosis are medically grounded—age is a crude proxy that can mislead.
  • Guard against bias: Once permitted, an age rule can widen over time, eroding trust in the health system.

POIs to test the other side

  • If two patients are equally likely to survive, why should age outrank a lottery?
  • Would you limit age to a tie-breaker when need and prognosis are identical?
  • How do you stop a bright-line age rule from becoming a hidden age cut-off?
  • Which clinical factors matter more than birthdays in your model?

Glossary — English → Swedish

Short definitions to support understanding. Use English terms in the debate.

triage → triage/prioritering — sortering av patienter i kris beroende på behov och nytta.

life-years → livsår — förväntade år som kan vinnas efter behandling.

prognosis → prognos — medicinsk bedömning av sannolikt utfall.

equal value principle → principen om lika värde — alla människor har samma värde; ålder i sig får inte avgöra vård.

greatest need first → störst behov först — den som riskerar mest skada ska få vården först.

frailty → skörhet — kroppen är skör och orkar inte återhämta sig lika snabbt.

tie-breaker → skiljekriterium — används för att skilja två patienter som annars är lika.

age cut-off → åldersgräns — fast gräns som nekar patienter på grund av ålder.

bright-line rule → tydligt avgränsad regel — klar ja/nej-regel utan gråzon.

strict safeguards → strikta skyddskrav — tydliga krav som gör att undantag bara används säkert.

burden of proof → bevisbörda — vem som måste visa att regeln är rättvis och nödvändig.

slippery slope → sluttande plan — risk att ett undantag gradvis utvidgas.

first-come, first-served → först till kvarn — ordningen man anländer avgör, oavsett behov.

priority list → prioritetslista — förbestämd ordning som avgör vem som behandlas först.
