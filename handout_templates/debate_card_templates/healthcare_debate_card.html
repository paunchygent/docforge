Debate Card — Age Priority in Health Care

Motion + prep facts on one page. English only here; Swedish glossary after the page break.

Motion

Should hospitals prioritize younger patients over older patients for life-saving treatment during a severe shortage?

Context

Swedish healthcare rests on two core principles: människovärdesprincipen (equal value of all lives) and behovsprincipen (greatest need first). During the COVID-19 pandemic, hospitals worldwide faced shortages of ICU beds and ventilators, forcing doctors to make impossible choices about who receives life-saving treatment.

The debate asks whether age may ever be a factor in triage decisions. Some argue it should serve as a tie-breaker when medical prognosis is identical, maximizing total life-years saved. Others argue that using age—even as a tie-breaker—violates equal dignity and opens the door to discrimination.

If the motion passed, Swedish hospitals could adopt formal protocols allowing age as a criterion during severe shortages. This would shift from purely clinical factors (prognosis, frailty, comorbidities) to include a demographic measure that proponents call fair and opponents call unacceptable.

Key questions

  • More life-years vs. equal respect: Should saving the most life-years outweigh treating all ages the same?
  • Rule clarity vs. clinical judgment: Is a clear rule ("younger first") better than bedside prognosis?
  • Tie-breaker or standalone rule: May age only act once need and prognosis are tied, or can it become a primary criterion?

YES — Prioritise the young

  • Maximise life-years: When equipment is scarce, giving it to those likely to live longer can increase total life-years saved.
  • Fairness to the young: Younger patients have not yet had the chance to experience education, career, family, or old age—using age as a tie-breaker gives them a fair opportunity to live a full life.
  • Prevents arbitrary outcomes: Without clear criteria, triage becomes a lottery or first-come, first-served—age provides a transparent, defensible standard when all else is equal.

NO — Keep age out of healthcare

  • Equal human dignity: The human dignity principle says age alone must never decide who gets care.
  • Clinical relevance first: Frailty, comorbidities, and prognosis are medically grounded—age is a crude proxy that can mislead.
  • Destroys public trust: If people fear they will be denied care based on age, they may avoid hospitals during crises or lose faith in the healthcare system's commitment to equal treatment.

POIs to test the other side

  • If two patients are equally likely to survive, why should age outrank a lottery?
  • Would you limit age to a tie-breaker when need and prognosis are identical?
  • How do you stop a bright-line age rule from becoming a hidden age cut-off?
  • Which clinical factors matter more than birthdays in your model?

Glossary — English → Swedish

Short definitions to support understanding. Use English terms in the debate.

triage → triage — snabb prioritering av patienter när intensivvårdsplatser eller respiratorer tar slut.

life-years → livsår — uppskattade år som räddas; exempelvis kan en 40-åring förväntas vinna fler år än en 80-åring.

prognosis → prognos — medicinsk bedömning av sannolikt utfall.

equal value principle → människovärdesprincipen — en grundprincip i svensk sjukvård som säger att alla människor har lika värde och samma rätt till vård, oavsett ålder.

greatest need first → behovsprincipen — principen att den med det största behovet av vård ska ges företräde.

frailty → medicinsk skörhet — ett tillstånd där en persons motståndskraft mot sjukdom och skada är nedsatt, vilket påverkar återhämtningen.

tie-breaker → skiljekriterium — extraregel som avgör när prognosen är identisk, till exempel att den som är yngst får respiratorn.

age cut-off → åldersgräns — fast gräns som nekar patienter på grund av ålder.

bright-line rule → tydlig regel med fast gräns — en klar och enkel regel som inte lämnar utrymme för tolkning.

strict safeguards → strikta säkerhetsåtgärder — kontroller som dokumenterade protokoll, beslut av flera läkare och extern tillsyn för att förhindra godtyckliga beslut.

burden of proof → bevisbörda — skyldigheten att bevisa att ett påstående eller kriterium är rättvist och motiverat.

slippery slope → risk för glidning — varning för att ett första steg kan leda till en kedja av oönskade konsekvenser.

first-come, first-served → först till kvarn — ordningen man anländer avgör, oavsett behov.

priority list → prioritetslista — förbestämd ordning som avgör vem som behandlas först.
