The Great Debate — Teacher Guide

Module outline for English teachers integrating UR’s The Great Debate series with the accompanying debate resources.

Purpose

This guide supports Swedish upper-secondary English classes in delivering structured debates. Lessons align UR’s televised coaching with our debate cards, student-facing handouts, judge rubric, vocabulary quizzes, and follow-up writing tasks.

Learning Objectives

  • Use the SEAL framework while fulfilling opener, builder, and closer speaking roles.
  • Handle Points of Information and live rebuttals clearly and confidently.
  • Apply rhetorical devices modelled in The Great Debate to student speeches.
  • Grow English debate vocabulary supported by Swedish civic context.
  • Deliver a full-length debate evaluated against transparent criteria and transfer learning to a timed written response.

Core Resources

  • UR Play: The Great Debate (Episodes 1–4)
  • UR Teachers Guide (PDF) for The Great Debate — teacher-facing resource alongside this guide.
  • Debate card collection — topic sheets with Swedish context, arguments, POIs, and glossaries.
  • Student-facing handouts — Student Debate Guide, Team Debate Preparation Sheet, Judge’s Score Card.
  • Timed essay prompt — teacher resource for post-debate written assessment.

Implementation Notes

Each lesson is planned for an 80-minute block. The sequence assumes two English lessons per week across five weeks. You may compress the schedule by setting episode viewing as homework and reducing in-class drill time while retaining the instructional phases listed below.

Ten-Lesson Planner

A condensed 10-lesson module with 80-minute lessons.
  • Lesson 1: Introduction & Core Concepts (Episode 1) — Introduce the module, assessment, and the Student Debate Guide. Watch Episode 1 and discuss debate norms and structure.
  • Lesson 2: Building Arguments (Episode 2) — Watch Episode 2. Workshop the SEAL method (Statement, Example, Argument, Link) using simple, non-controversial examples.
  • Lesson 3: Clash & Interruption (Episode 3) — Watch Episode 3. Practice handling Points of Information (POIs) and structuring rebuttals using the “They said… / We disagree… / Because… / Therefore…” model.
  • Lesson 4: Topic Assignment & Analysis (Episode 4) — Watch Episode 4, focusing on delivery. Teacher assigns teams and Debate Cards. Teams analyze their card, mapping arguments for both sides.
  • Lesson 5: Argument Drafting Workshop — Teams draft a pool of strong SEAL arguments for both sides of their motion. Teacher holds coaching conferences to check for logical soundness.
  • Lesson 6: Dress Rehearsal & Position Assignment — Conduct mini-debates with temporarily assigned sides. After peer feedback using the Judge’s Score Card, final Affirmative/Negative positions are assigned.
  • Lesson 7: Final Case Preparation — With sides now fixed, teams complete a single Debate Prep Planner, finalize their thesis, select arguments, and script their speeches.
  • Lessons 8-10: Final Debates & Assessment — This 3-lesson block is for full-length debates. Non-participating students judge. The Timed Op-Ed Assignment is briefed as a follow-up task.

Assessment

Formative assessment consists of glossary quizzes linked to the bilingual word lists and verbal feedback delivered after dress rehearsals. Summative assessment may include debate performance (with accompanying verbal feedback), Judge’s Score Card submissions to verify core knowledge, and an individual timed essay on the debate topic using the provided prompt.
