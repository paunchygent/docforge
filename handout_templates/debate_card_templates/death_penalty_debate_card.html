Debate Card — Death Penalty

Motion + prep facts on one page. English only here; Swedish glossary after the page break.

Motion

Should the death penalty be legal only when guilt is proven beyond reasonable doubt with strong forensic evidence?

Context

Sweden bans the death penalty in law and treaties; this motion imagines a narrow legal exception.

Swedish criminal courts apply "beyond reasonable doubt" in practice; "strong forensic evidence" means DNA with secure chain of custody or validated methods plus independent corroboration.

Today the maximum penalty is life imprisonment; introducing capital punishment would demand new sentencing, appeal, and prison procedures.

Key questions

  • Full ban vs narrow exception: Keep a total prohibition, or allow a strictly limited rule?
  • Near-certainty vs any risk: If evidence is very strong, is that enough, or is any execution risk unacceptable?
  • Public safety vs limits on state power: Would this penalty improve safety, or should the state never take life?
  • Fixed gate vs discretion: Bright-line forensic gate, or case-by-case judicial judgment?

YES — Allow death penalty with rock-solid proof

  • Proportionate justice: In Sweden's most brutal murders, a death sentence could match the harm and give families moral closure beyond life terms.
  • Deters planned atrocities: A narrowly available death penalty signals decisive consequences for premeditated terrorism or killings of police and officials.
  • Protects society: Executing the worst offenders removes risks of escapes, prison violence, or directing networks from Swedish prisons.

NO — Keep Sweden's ban even with strong forensics

  • Irreversible risk: Forensic methods still have error rates; an execution cannot be undone.
  • Forensic fallibility: Contamination, analyst errors, or lab misconduct have produced wrongful convictions even with DNA, so the risk fails the beyond-reasonable-doubt test.
  • State restraint principle: Sweden's justice model centres on human dignity and rehabilitation; authorising executions would expand state power in ways the legal culture rejects.

POIs to test the other side

  • If a premeditated mass murder has DNA, video, and admissions, why is life imprisonment still proportionate or protective enough?
  • What alternative ensures those who orchestrate killings from prison can never endanger police or witnesses again?
  • Which Swedish safeguards would catch lab contamination or analyst bias before an execution happens?
  • How does giving the state power to kill align with Sweden's human-rights leadership abroad?

Glossary — English → Swedish

Short definitions to support understanding. Use English terms in the debate.

beyond reasonable doubt → bortom rimligt tvivel — högsta beviskravet i svenska brottmål.

strong forensic evidence → starka forensiska bevis — tillförlitliga analyser (t.ex. DNA) med dokumenterad beviskedja.

DNA evidence → DNA-bevis — genetiskt spår som kopplar en person till brottsplatsen.

chain of custody → beviskedja — logg som visar vem som hanterat beviset från fynd till domstol.

deterrence → avskräckning — idé att hårda straff ska hindra andra från att begå liknande brott.

wrongful conviction → felaktig dom — när en person fälls för brott trots att hen är oskyldig.

burden of proof → bevisbörda — vilken part som måste visa att något stämmer och hur starkt.

bright-line rule → tydligt avgränsad regel — klar ja/nej-regel utan gråzon.

strict safeguards → strikta skyddskrav — tydliga krav som håller dödsstraffet inom en snäv ram.

slippery slope → sluttande plan — risk att ett undantag gradvis utvidgas.

rule of law → rättsstatens principer — samma tydliga lagar och processer för alla.

life without parole → livstid utan möjlighet till frigivning — fängelse på livstid utan chans till villkorlig frigivning.

retributive justice → vedergällning — idén att straff ska spegla brottets allvar och ge moralisk balans.
