Timed Op-Ed Assignment

Write for the international student section of the Swedish daily Nordic Outlook.

Assignment Brief

Nordic Outlook runs a monthly feature where upper-secondary students in Sweden share perspectives on current policy debates for peers abroad. You have been invited to contribute an op-ed on the motion assigned to your debate team. Choose the stance you believe in most strongly and craft a persuasive piece for readers aged 16–20 who follow Swedish affairs from outside the country.

Length: 400–600 words. Time limit: 80 minutes. You may consult the debate card for your topic while writing; no other notes or sources are permitted.

Core Requirements

  • Lead with a hook that situates the issue in everyday Swedish life or policy news.
  • State your position clearly and support it with two well-developed arguments grounded in facts, reasoning, or examples relevant to Swedish society.
  • Acknowledge a credible counter-view and explain why your stance offers a stronger way forward for students, families, or institutions.
  • Conclude with a forward-looking insight or invitation to discussion that resonates with an international student audience.

Planning Prompts

Use the prompts below to map your op-ed before you begin drafting:

  • Which pieces of evidence or framing from your preparation (debate card, research notes) best establish your viewpoint?
  • How does this topic connect to larger themes in Sweden—such as equality, innovation, public health, or democratic participation?
  • What perspective might a Swedish reader abroad or an exchange student find surprising or insightful?
  • Which sentence will you end with to leave the reader thinking about action or further inquiry?

Submission Checklist

  • Word count between 400 and 600 words.
  • You may consult the debate card while drafting; do not use any other materials.
  • Op-ed submitted to the teacher/editor at the end of the 80-minute session.
  • Language should remain formal yet engaging, using precise vocabulary and purposeful connectors.
