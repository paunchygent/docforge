Debate Card — Survival Cannibalism

Motion + prep facts on one page. English only here; Swedish glossary after the page break.

Motion

Should Swedish law excuse the consumption of human remains in extreme survival situations where no killing occurs?

Context

In rare disasters, people have survived by eating those who had already died. This raises a difficult choice: is keeping someone alive more important than protecting the dignity of the dead and the peace of the grave?

Swedish law also discusses necessity (acting in an emergency to stop serious harm) and strong protections for grave peace.

Key questions

  • Life vs dignity after death: Does saving a living person outweigh respect for a dead person's body?
  • Clear rules vs mercy: Should the law set a strict rule, or should rare cases be handled with clemency after the fact?
  • Proof: How can anyone show it was truly the only way to survive?

YES — Excuse it when no killing occurs

  • Life first: No further person is harmed; survival should come before rules about the dead.
  • Possible consent: Some people would allow their body to be used to save others.
  • Narrow exception: A tiny, clearly limited rule avoids punishing honest survivors.

NO — Do not excuse it

  • Respect for the dead: Law protects graves and human remains for families and society.
  • Hard to verify: After rescue it is difficult to prove there was no other way; risk of abuse.
  • Public trust: Allowing exceptions can normalise extreme actions and damage trust.

POIs to test the other side

  • If the deceased left explicit consent, why should the state override that choice to save a life?
  • When death was hours away, what principle justifies punishing survivors who harmed no one?
  • What proof would convince you that every other food source was exhausted before turning to remains?
  • If the law allows an exception, how will you verify crews rationed stores and signalled for rescue before invoking it?

Glossary — English → Swedish

Short definitions to support understanding. Use English terms in the debate.

sanctity of life → livets okränkbarhet / livets helgd — Idén att livet ska skyddas först.

dignity after death → värdighet efter döden / gravfrid — Respekt för den döde och kroppen.

rule of law → rättsstatens principer — Samma tydliga regler för alla.

clemency → nåd — Förlåtelse eller lindring från staten efter dom.

necessity → nöd (BrB 24:4) — Handling i nödläge för att avvärja allvarlig fara.

consent → samtycke — Tydligt ja till att något får göras.

bright-line rule → tydligt avgränsad regel — klar ja/nej-regel utan gråzon.

burden of proof → bevisbörda — Vem som måste visa att något stämmer.

slippery slope → sluttande plan — risk att ett undantag gradvis utvidgas.

strict safeguards → strikta skyddskrav — Tydliga krav som begränsar undantag och håller dem säkra.

human remains → mänskliga kvarlevor — Kropp efter döden.

grave peace → gravfrid — Skydd för gravar och kvarlevor.

indefensible / disproportionate → oförsvarligt — Inte rimligt i förhållande till risken eller skadan.
